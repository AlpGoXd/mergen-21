# Manufacturing Defect — Concave Horn Walls

The aluminum sheet used to build the horn antenna was obtained at no cost because it had been bent during manufacturing. This resulted in the horn walls being **concave** (bowed inward) along the length of the antenna, rather than perfectly flat.

## Evidence

- `concavity_photo.jpg` — Photo showing the inward curvature of the horn walls

## Impact

The concavity deforms the intended pyramidal horn geometry, which may cause:
- Reduced aperture efficiency due to non-planar phase distribution at the aperture
- Lower realized gain compared to the ideal CST simulation
- Minor beam pattern distortion / asymmetry
